Cauliflower polenta balls with vegan chili yogurt

Cauliflower polenta balls offer virtually the optimal vegetable hiding place. They consist of cauliflower shredded in the food processor and creamy, deliciously spiced polenta. By the way, the fine corn semolina is suitable for so much more than just a quick, smooth side dish. Even if these only prepared with a little salt and pepper inspire us again and again - whether fried mushrooms, braised chard or a simple tomato sauce. Here, however, it now acts as a tasty counterpart to the cauliflower grated and also ensures the good grip and pleasant bite of our vegetable balls. An extra touch of polenta as a sheath conjures up a crispy shell when frying, inside they remain tender and creamy.

Ingredients

Preparation time 15 min
Working hours 30 min

For the cauliflower polenta ball

  • 425 ml water
  • 175 g polenta
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp paprika powder
  • 1 tbsp yeast flakes
  • 1‚ÅÑ2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 200 g cauliflower
  • 60 g corn
  • 2 tbsp corn starch
  • 3 tbsp olive oil for frying

For the chili yogurt

  • 25 g chili
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 250 g yogurt

In additon

  • 15g dill

  1. Water to a boil. Stir in 125 g of polenta, salt, paprika powder, yeast flakes and pepper with a whisk and simmer for 1 minute over medium to low heat while stirring. Turn off the stove and let it swell for 5 minutes with the lid closed
  2. Grate garlic, finely chop cauliflower in the food processor
  3. Stir garlic, cauliflower, corn and corn starch into the polenta
  4. Polenta-vegetable mixture into 20 golf ball-sized balls, turn in the rest of the polenta and fry in a hot, coated pan with oil on all sides for 5-6 minutes over medium to high heat until golden brown until crispy
  5. For the chili yogurt, put all the ingredients in the food processor and puree
  6. Cauliflower polenta balls on chili yoghurt and serve topped with dill and optionally topped with quickly pickled red onion cubes (see tip)

Tips

If you don't have any onion pickles in stock, just cut half a red onion into fine cubes and mix it with three tablespoons of vinegar.
